# Response to Reviewers

## 1. Gravity / BSSN / Nonlinear Closure

**Reviewer's Objection:** "The manuscript establishes, at most, compatibility with selected weak-field observables. The fluid-to-metric map remains post-Newtonian and analogical; extra fluid modes should generically survive and spoil canonical GR. The BSSN-EKG simulations merely place a scalar into GR and therefore do not derive Einstein gravity from the fluid."

**Author's Response:** This objection does not engage the full structure of Part II. The manuscript's gravity claim does not rest on BSSN simulations alone, nor on phenomenological matching alone.

First, §9.3.1 gives the explicit nonlinear mechanism: acoustic backreaction modifies the metric through second-order density shifts, and the advective nonlinearity $(\mathbf{v} \cdot \nabla)\mathbf{v}$ is mapped term-by-term to the quadratic $\Gamma\Gamma$ structure of the Ricci tensor. The manuscript is explicit that the Christoffel-symbol products are generated by the same mathematical nonlinearity that appears in the Euler equation.

Second, the putative "extra mode" contamination is addressed formally in §9.3.15 through the Geometric Higgs mechanism. The three Goldstone modes associated with broken Galilean boosts are not left as propagating spurious vectors. They are absorbed into the emergent vierbein, becoming the gravitomagnetic sector $h_{0i}$. After local Lorentz redundancy and the first-class diffeomorphism constraints are imposed, the low-energy gravity sector retains the correct propagating content rather than an uncontrolled scalar-vector residue.

Third, and decisively, §9.3.16 supplies the canonical closure that the objection says is missing. There the fluid Hamiltonian and momentum density constraints are shown to satisfy the exact Hypersurface Deformation Algebra of GR. The structure functions are the inverse emergent spatial metric $q^{ij}$. This is not merely a weak-field analogy; it is the canonical GR constraint algebra realized in the fluid variables themselves.

The role of the BSSN-EKG simulations is therefore narrower and physical, not formal: they demonstrate that once the canonically closed emergent gravity sector is coupled to the condensate scalar, the quantum-pressure term regularizes collapse in the core while preserving a GR-like exterior branch. The derivational burden is carried by the nonlinear map, the geometric Higgs mechanism, and the exact HDA closure; the simulations exhibit nonlinear realization of that branch rather than substituting for the canonical proof.

## 2. CKM / Numerology / Overlap-Integral Identification

**Reviewer's Objection:** "The CKM result remains numerological. Even if r/R is a true extremum of the energy functional, the identification of that geometry with the Cabibbo angle is an interpretation map. The overlap integral is merely geometric unless one proves that it is the actual weak-transition amplitude."

**Author's Response:** The manuscript removes arbitrariness from the CKM construction in two distinct ways, but the revised submission also states clearly that this remains part of Extension Module A, not a theorem of Parts I–II.

First, the geometric input is not fitted. In §9.3.26a, the torus aspect ratio $u=r/R$ is fixed by the unique minimum of the dimensionless energy functional, yielding $u^* = 1/\sqrt{2\pi^2} = 0.225079\ldots$ with numerical confirmation reported in the manuscript. The ratio is therefore internally fixed by the GP-plus-torsional energetics of the medium, not chosen to reproduce flavor data.

Second, the nonzero overlap is not produced by an arbitrary repair term. The framework is explicitly viscoelastic. The torsional elastic contribution already appears in the same energy functional whose minimization fixes $r/R$, so the torsional phase is constitutive to the medium rather than appended post hoc for flavor matching. In the logic of the framework, a medium without this torsional sector would not support the transverse-wave content used elsewhere in the theory.

Third, the operator-level identification is supplied by the vortex-reconnection sector. In §9.3.25, reconnection processes define the non-commutative topological dynamics of the defect sector. Within that structure, a generation-changing event is modeled as a reconnection/phase-slip process between topological defect states. The natural quantum matrix element for such a process is therefore the overlap of the corresponding defect-core wavefunctions. The manuscript's overlap formalism is not introduced as a decorative dictionary; it is the native amplitude prescription of the reconnection dynamics.

That said, the revised manuscript itself now classifies §9.3.26 as a topological ansatz and states explicitly that the CKM knot assignments are postulated rather than derived from a unique GP selection rule. What the manuscript does establish is that this sector is not arbitrary numerology: it is a constrained, internally structured viscoelastic-topological construction built on a fixed energy minimum and a geometric transition formalism.

## 3. Epistemic Framing & Core EFT Closure

**Reviewer's Objection:** "The framework should be downgraded from derivation to compatibility ansatz. The manuscript oscillates between strong derivational language and a retreat into 'exploratory module' language, undermining the credibility of the claimed closure."

**Author's Response:** The distinction is deliberate, not evasive, and we do not agree with a blanket downgrade to "compatibility ansatz."

Parts I and II now constitute the core EFT sector. Part II's title, abstract, and theorem boundaries state that the claims are restricted to the macroscopic infrared regime and do not assert an exact UV-complete reconstruction. Within that stated regime, however, the manuscript provides formal closure of the gravity sector through the nonlinear fluid-to-curvature map, geometric Higgs mode absorption, and exact closure of the canonical constraints onto the HDA of GR. Those are stronger than a mere compatibility ansatz within the stated EFT regime.

Part III is now explicitly labeled Extension Module A, and its abstract and Axiom of Scope state that its contents are exploratory correspondences that do not modify, extend, or weaken any theorem established in Parts I and II. The manuscript specifically labels the octonionic sector as an embedding hypothesis, the $b_0=11$ recovery as a topological heuristic, and the CKM sector as a topological ansatz.

The current version makes a sharp epistemic partition:

- **Core formal closure** in Parts I–II.
- **Exploratory but internally constrained correspondences** in Part III.

This distinction is deliberate and prevents speculative topological extensions from being confused with the theorem-bearing canonical structure of the core EFT.
